Breast Cancer

 

Knowledge

Breast cancer caused 670 000 deaths globally in 2022. Roughly half of all breast cancers occur in women with no specific risk factors other than sex and age. Breast cancer was the most common cancer in women in 157 countries out of 185 in 2022. Breast cancer occurs in every country in the world. Approximately 0.5–1% of breast cancers occur in men.

 

Overview

Breast cancer is a disease in which abnormal breast cells grow out of control and form tumours. If left unchecked, the tumours can spread throughout the body and become fatal. Breast cancer cells begin inside the milk ducts and/or the milk-producing lobules of the breast. The earliest form (in situ) is not life-threatening and can be detected in early stages. Cancer cells can spread into nearby breast tissue (invasion). This creates tumours that cause lumps or thickening. Invasive cancers can spread to nearby lymph nodes or other organs (metastasize). Metastasis can be life-threatening and fatal. Treatment is based on the person, the type of cancer and its spread. Treatment combines surgery, radiation therapy and medications.

 

About the Project

 

Binary diagnostic classification system

for breast cancer

The aim of this machine learning project was to develop a diagnostic tumor classification system that assigns the classes “benign” and “malignant” based on a dataset. The training dataset is composed of data from 381 patients. The data comes from examining the breast tissue of the individuals, where the cell nuclei were detected. The findings from the examination were divided into characteristics such as radius, area, concavity, etc. Through calculations the characteristics were then split in the three features (maximum, minimum and mean). In total, there are 10 characteristics and accordingly 30 features. These features are represented as columns in the dataset. So, a row is the data of one person.

The following page shows an overview of the individual patients with the corresponding diagnosis. The diagnosis -1 means 'cancer-positive', the diagnosis 1 means 'no cancer'. By clicking on the corresponding names you can see the exact results of the individual cell examinations. You can also delete persons or change their names, ages, etc...
